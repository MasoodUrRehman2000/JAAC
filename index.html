Understanding The Crisis

A Briefing for the International Community

The Forgotten Region

While the global community often views Kashmir strictly through the lens of India-Pakistan border disputes, the internal struggles of the people living in Pakistani-administered Kashmir (often referred to locally as Azad Jammu & Kashmir) are frequently overlooked. This region is currently facing severe economic marginalization and a stark deficit of civil liberties.

Who are the JAAC?

The Jammu Kashmir Joint Awami Action Committee (JAAC) is not a militant or separatist organization. It is a peaceful, grassroots civil-society coalition uniting local traders, transporters, lawyers, students, and everyday citizens. They mobilized to challenge the political status quo and advocate for economic survival.

The Spark of the Movement

Triggered by crushing inflation, manipulated power tariffs on electricity produced in their own region, and a lack of local governance, the people took to the streets in peaceful protest. The state's response has involved internet blackouts, military crackdowns, and the unlawful detention of civil leaders.

The 38-Point Charter of Demands

A roadmap to economic fairness, structural reform, and dignity.

  • Subsidies on wheat flour prices equivalent to Gilgit-Baltistan to combat extreme inflation.
  • Reduced power tariffs calculated strictly on the production costs of local hydro-projects (e.g., Mangla Dam).
  • Abolition of excessive perks, luxuries, and privileges enjoyed by the political elite and bureaucracy.
  • Removal of 12 seats in the AJK Legislative Assembly reserved for Pakistan-based refugees to ensure fair regional representation.
  • Criminal cases to be registered under appropriate provisions for incidents of violence/vandalism.
  • Compensation for families of those killed equal to that of law-enforcement martyrs.
  • Financial compensation of Rs 1 million allocated for injured persons.
  • One family member of each deceased to be given government employment within 20 days.
  • Local Government Act to be brought in line with the 1990 Act within 90 days.
  • Immediate release of funds for the implementation of the health card programme.
  • MRI and CT scan machines to be provided to each district hospital phase-wise.
  • Allocation of Rs 10 billion for upgrading the regional electricity grid and system.
  • Implementation of the 2019 High Court decision regarding hydel power projects and royalty.
  • Size of the regional cabinet to be permanently reduced to a maximum of 20 members.
  • Administrative secretaries to be strictly limited to 20 personnel.
  • Civil Defence Department to be merged with the State Disaster Management Authority (SDMA).
  • Operation theatres and nurseries to be fully funded for all Tehsil Headquarters Hospitals.
  • Taxes on property transfer to be brought at par with neighboring provinces within three months.
  • Advance tax rates to be reduced in line with Gilgit-Baltistan and former FATA areas.
  • Admissions to educational institutions to follow open merit without undue quota restrictions.
  • FIRs for incidents at Banjosa, Muzaffarabad, and other districts to be investigated by a Judicial Commission.
  • Unconditional release of all arrested leaders and political detainees.
  • Immediate end to the security crackdown and lifting of military blockades.
  • Immediate and uninterrupted restoration of internet and mobile phone communication services.
  • Guaranteed uninterrupted supply of essential food, flour, and medicines to all districts.
  • Withdrawal of additional military and police personnel deployed during recent unrest.
  • Immediate unfreezing of all bank accounts belonging to JAAC leaders and activists.
  • Return of all seized identity documents and passports to peaceful activists.
  • Establishment of an independent monitoring committee for all infrastructure projects.
  • Provision of free, accessible, and quality basic education across all rural districts.
  • Infrastructure development including new tunnels and bridges connecting mountainous regions.
  • Immediate operationalization and funding of the Muzaffarabad international airport.
  • Creation of improved local employment opportunities to combat regional poverty.
  • Full devolution of authority to local governments to ensure regional resource ownership.
  • Withdrawal of all legal cases/FIRs filed against peaceful activists during 2023-2026 protests.
  • Opening of the Line of Control (LoC) for humanitarian assistance if food/medicine shortages persist.
  • Implementation of transparent, digital voter lists to secure fair electoral representation.
  • Execution of all previously agreed-upon relief measures without further administrative delay.
